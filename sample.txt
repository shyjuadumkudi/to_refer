The "Father of Artificial Intelligence" is widely considered to be John McCarthy.

Who was John McCarthy?
An American computer scientist and cognitive scientist.

Coined the term "Artificial Intelligence" in 1956.

Organized the Dartmouth Conference in 1956, which is considered the birth of AI as a field.

His Contributions:
Developed the LISP programming language, which became the standard AI language for decades.

Pioneered work in time-sharing systems and early AI planning.

So, while many scientists contributed to the development of AI, John McCarthy is most commonly credited as its father.
